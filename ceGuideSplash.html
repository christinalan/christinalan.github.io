Underground Topography

A simulation of underground club culture as a form of historical re-enactment

The underground is a liminal space. Its existence is contingent on its nonexistence in outside reality, a befitting playground for reinvention. While the appearances of simulation and historical re-enactment differ, the search for meaning through bodily navigation remains the same. Historical re-enactment is a simulation, a conversation between an active past and a speculative future. Our memories are conduits for determining new ways of being.

Underground club culture gives us the opportunity to explore how our body remembers. Devoid of fixity and permanence, the undoing of boundaries interlaces with a renewed sense of belonging. Possibilities become driven by perceptual experience, and we return yet again to these spaces to re-imagine how to live. This project is an attempt to recreate the underground's liminal playground through a stratified study of its unique conditions. With or without the context of a global pandemic, the hope for this project is to offer participants an opportunity to heal, reflect, and re-invent themselves through an experience of emancipatory possibility.

This experience is best when used with headphones. Please settle into a space with limited distractions and enough room for movement or stillness. If you have multiple devices, you can trigger audio samples on each device for a more full-bodied experience.
